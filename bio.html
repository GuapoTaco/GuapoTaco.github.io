  • N
    • 1N means 1 set of chromosomes. Most cells are 2N, one copy from each parent.
  • Haploid
    • 1N
  • Diploid
    • 2N
  • Centromere
    • Link part in between the sister chromatids
  • Centrosome
    • A group of centriles and a membrane around them

  • Centrioles

    • Something that holds microtubles that are in the mitotic spindle

    The green cylinders are Centrioles, everything inside the dotted circle (including the dottted circle) is a Centrisome

  • cell cycle (normal cells that do mitosis)

    • interphase = copies DNA and prepares for mitosis

      • G1
        • gap 1
        • cell grows and functions normally
      • G0
        • Normal function
        • Optional
        • No growth, no DNA synthesis. The cell goes into G0 unitl it is ready to divide.

      • Synthesis (S)

        • duplicates DNA, cell should be 4N afterwards
      • G2
        • gap 2
        • resumes growth
        • mithocondria divide
        • chloroplasts divide (if present)
    • Prophase
      • Chromatin condenses into chromosomes
      • Nucleolus disapperas
    • Mitotic phase (M)
      • Prometaphase
        • Nuclear membrane dies hard
        • Move centrosomes apart
      • Metaphase
        • Chromosomes line up along metaphase plate (not a real plate)
    • Anaphse
      • Chromosomes break at centromeres
    • Telophase
      • Nuclear membrane reforms
      • Nucleoli reappear (2 of them)
      • Chromosomes unwind
    • Cytokinesis
      • When the cells split into two seperate (2N) cells
  • Spindle fibers
    • Seperate the chromosomes during Anaphase of Telophase.
    • Attached to Centrioles
  • Cell-cycle control
    • After G1 if the cell isn't supposed to divide it will go into G0
  • Metastasis
    • Spread of Cancer or other disease to a non-attached place
    • Goes through blood
  • Meiosis (replaces mitosis in the previous model)
    • Prophase I
      • Crossing over, pairing up.
    • Metaphase I
      • Chromosomes line up
    • Anaphase I
      • Chromosomes pulled apart
    • Telephase I & Cytokinesis
      • Split
    • Prophase II
      • Nuclear envolope breaks down
    • Metaphase II
      • Chromosomes line up
    • Anaphase II
      • Sister chromatids pulled apart
    • Telophase II & Cytokinesis
      • Split again
  • Karyotype
    • Test to see if your chromosomes are good to go
  • Non-disjunction
    • Too few or too many chromosomes in meiosis
  • Inversion
    • When a segment is inverted end to end
